LOGEMENTS

From the middle of the 19th century, the transformation of the city of Paris by the great renovation under Haussmann led to the creation of hollow teeth. These small unexploited plots surrounded by gable walls mark a break in the continuity of the bati. To stop the densification of the city of Paris, new constructions will appear to meet the constant need for housing.

The project we have dealt with this semester is located at 6-8 rue Bardinet in the 14th arrondissement of Paris. The building is in a small plot very constrained by the opposite but Through the configuration of this portion of territory, the objective was to design living spaces offering direct openings to the two most interesting points of view of the project: the green garden and the square located at the intersection of the street. The volumetry of the project resulted from an extraction of the shaded part on the plot to develop the areas exposed to sunlight. The project evolves around these two directions that orient the openings on the site, allowing to limit the opposite while having an optimal brightness through an east-west exposure. this community consists of seven floors, each serving three apartments. The vertical circulation appears as the central point of spatial organization with a distribution of dwellings around a common landing. The ground floor is designed to think collective through a cafe and kitchen shared by the residents of the building. The exterior marks an access of this small private courtyard to the large garden shared with the other plots.
